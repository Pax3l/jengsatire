Teachers do not teach, according to students

November 15, 2016

By Kristel Fung

The quality of teachers at HKIS has become a frequently discussed issue. As the years pass, more and more students have begun to question whether the education they are receiving is up to standard.

In difficult classes such as Honors Advanced Algebra and Honors Physics, many are complaining that their teachers do not teach the material well though. Those who do not receive good grades are especially vocal, and have begun to speak out about the lack of learning done in class.

Students struggling to understand material in class

A senior who took Honors Advanced Algebra his sophomore year says, "[My teacher] didn't teach. Like, no one in class got the concepts and she just didn't explain things well. That's why I did so badly." Interestingly enough, the student moved up from regular Geometry to Honors. However, it's unreasonable for the teacher to expect that the student will understand the material as quickly as the normal pace of the class. The curriculum should change to suit the students' needs, as that is what edcuation is for.

"I don’t understand. The test was so hard and I got a C, it’s obviously [the teacher's] fault because he didn’t get that I was confused and he didn’t come to me and ask if I understood everything. There’s nothing I could have done myself," says a freshman who is currently taking Honors Chemistry.

“I basically bombed the last two tests because everything in class is just so confusing. It’s like she expects us to understand things instantly when concepts are presented to us.” When the student was asked if they did their homework, the student declined to comment.

There is a coincidential correlation between those complaining about their teachers and those who do not do their homework and do not get good grades. But of course, this is entirely unrelated and it is ultimately the teacher's fault for not giving enough support to their students.

“I don’t understand why [the teacher] was hired. No wonder everyone in this class is failing - we can’t be expected to work hard and do all our homework on our own. Like we just don’t have the time, especially with all these sports and extracurriculars," says a student taking Honors Biology.

Students cannot be expected to be doing all their homework, especially since homework assignments have been made formative. Teachers should be the ones to blame if students need to spend extra effort outside of class to succeed.

In addition to this, even with immense outside resources such as the internet, parents, and peers, students are rightfully outraged at the lack of guidance when they are expected to study on their own and understand concepts. One particularly angry student says, "Like what are we expected to do? Go online and search things up to teach ourselves? No, that’s what teachers are for. You’d think that after paying so much money through tuition, they’d have some good teachers at this place.”

If HKIS wants to seriously consider themselves as one of the top schools in Hong Kong, they should definitely be looking more closely at who they hire. As of now, the standard has been set far too low and improvements have to be made immediately.
